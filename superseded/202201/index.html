ABOUT THE PRACTICE

cllctv was formed out of a growing need for basic research in the design, planning, and construction industries to address climate resiliency and equity in the built environment.

focusing on discrete processes that value real, testable outcomes while promoting increased project clarity through visualizations; all cllctv projects aim to improve the means and methods in which projects are delivered - leveraging multiple skill sets to ensure cost and time efficacy during preliminary stages, reduce changes once the project has been initiated, and limit the impact that our projects have on existing natural systems.

basic research

cllctv projects are first treated as questions of basic research. basic research emphasizes the exploration of ideas vs the finding of solutions to specific problems - in the context of this practice, that is defined by attempting to "understand the problem before trying to solve the problem." this methodology can take many forms, but generally involves conducting stakeholder surveys, completing high-level budgets and schedules early in the design process, mitigating the need to reconcile them as the design is nearing completion.

discrete processes

all projects are a collection of tasks and associated due by dates, a fact that offers an opportunity to 'test' the design at each of those junctures. cllctv's process emphasizes those testable tasks as a way to efficiently iterate on the design, using basic research methodologies to determine if the project has maintained its intent, or if some course correction is neccessary.

project clarity

no project is an island - whether it is stakeholder input, funding requirements, or the community at large; much of a projects success depends on its ability to clearly convey its intent - by visualizing the processes used to deliver a project (schedules, reports, etc) we make the project more accessible, encouraging meaningful input and preventing project changes due to miscommunication or lack of engagement.
