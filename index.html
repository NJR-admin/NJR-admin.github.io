OUR MISSION

To support Recovery from Addiction through a Holistic and Non-Judgemental approach to Wellness, along the full continuum of community-based support services - from Outreach and Education to Employment and Housing.


Non-Judgmental Recovery's services include:

OUTREACH

NJR's outreach is divided into three distinct programs:


  • the Rapid Recovery Response program provides on call referall services to individuals in need of housing, support while transitioning into treatment or between regulated facilities; including transport and application assistance;
  • the care package program includes the making and supply of care packs for the community, wellness checks, sorting and disbursement of community donations, and on-call support; and
  • NJR meetings are regular hybrid recovery meetings meant to foster non-judgemental support networks for recovering addicts - see our facebook page for more information.

EDUCATION

providing education to practitioners, support workers, volunteers, and the general community:


  • narcan training
  • training for volunteers (first aid, ...)*
  • presentations related to field work, research partnerships

EMPLOYMENT

Assistance, Opportunity, Training to help communitty members succeed, wherever they're at in their journey:


  • Referalls and Resume/application assistance
  • paid work opportunities*
  • Training and Mentorship opportunities*
  • Volunteer opportunities

HOUSING

Support, Referalls services to assist individuals in need of housing:


  • Referalls (see Rapid Recovery Response)
  • 2nd Stage Housing*
  • Service Connections and Assistance

*program in development or planned for future development


A Universal Approach to Recovery




The Non-Judgmental Recovery Society believes that there is value in all Recovery Programs. We have seen evidence among our peers that successful, long-term Recovery is more closely tied to the Connections and Support Systems one builds than to the specific Recovery Program one chooses to follow. We believe that there are Universal Principles that are essential in Recovery and that these Principles can be presented and discussed for their merits without being tied to any specific Program.
